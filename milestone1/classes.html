In Dungeons & Dragons, a class defines a character's abilities, strengths, and role within the adventuring party. Below are the core classes available in most editions of the game:

Barbarian

Fierce warriors of primitive background who can enter a battle rage. Barbarians excel in melee combat and have high durability.

Bard

Versatile spellcasters and musicians who inspire allies and manipulate magic through performance. Bards can heal, support, and fight.

Cleric

Divine spellcasters who serve a deity. Clerics can heal wounds, cure diseases, and wield powerful holy magic.

Druid

Guardians of nature with the ability to cast spells and transform into animals. Druids command the forces of the natural world.

Fighter

Masters of martial combat, skilled with a variety of weapons and armor. Fighters are adaptable and reliable in battle.

Monk

Martial artists who harness the power of their bodies and minds. Monks use unarmed strikes and special techniques.

Paladin

Holy warriors bound by an oath. Paladins combine martial prowess with divine magic to protect and heal.

Ranger

Skilled hunters and trackers, rangers are adept at surviving in the wild and fighting with ranged or melee weapons.

Rogue

Stealthy and dexterous, rogues excel at sneaking, disarming traps, and striking precisely for extra damage.

Sorcerer

Spellcasters who draw magic from innate power. Sorcerers have fewer spells but can cast them more flexibly.

Warlock

Magic users who gain their abilities through pacts with powerful entities. Warlocks have unique spells and invocations.

Wizard

Scholars of arcane magic with the widest selection of spells. Wizards prepare spells from their spellbooks each day.
